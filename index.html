Our Values

The principles that guide our work and define our approach

Community

We’re rooted in the local ski scene. From rail jams at local mountains to premieres across BC, we contribute to the community that shaped us.

Progression

Not just bigger tricks, but progression in how we film, travel, collaborate, and represent modern freeski culture.

Authenticity

CHEF has never been about trends or clout. We stay true to our vision, letting the skiing speak for itself without unnecessary embellishment.

Creativity

We approach skiing and filmmaking with our own perspective, drawing influence from skate culture and DIY film roots, staying true to our style.

Consistency

From annual full-length films to our shared work ethic on smaller projects, we show up every year and put in the time to make something meaningful.

Humility

We don’t overhype ourselves. We let the skiing and the work speak for itself, keeping the vibe grounded and approachable.

Who We Are

Since 2016, CHEF has functioned as a group of friends: a core crew of skiers always open to including new people in the filming process. What started at Grouse Mountain has steadily expanded, taking us to new locations and allowing us to broaden the scope of what we create.

Our three full-length videos have all been featured at iF3, with two riders nominated for Amateur Skier of the Year. We've grown a strong following on YouTube and were recently featured in the 4th issue of Browser ski magazine.

Our Creative Approach

Our Style

We always aim to pair quality music with clean, unpretentious cinematography, letting the skiing speak for itself without unnecessary drama.

Our "less is more" approach resonates with audiences who appreciate genuine content over overproduced spectacle.

Our Production Process

Filming Approach

All footage is captured on Panasonic HVX200 and AG-UX180 cameras, combining proven visual styles from skate cinematography with our own unique perspective. By avoiding over-produced aerials, voiceovers, or unnecessary effects, we focus on showcasing the skiing and the environment authentically, creating a style that is engaging, genuine, and true to our vision.

Post-Production

Editing is done in Adobe Premiere Pro, where we handle color grading, sound design, timing, and overall visual direction. Addition visuals will be done on Blender and After Effects

Location & Production Logistics

We film primarily in BC, from street spots to backcountry zones, and everywhere in between. Our crew knows the terrain, conditions, and timing needed for efficient sessions.

What's next for CHEF

Our next full-length video

We're in the planning stage of our next full-length video. We want Peak Performance to be a part of it!

We want to help Peak Performance gain visibility inside the community that defines the sport. Rather than a polished studio production, videos are rooted in real skiing, real friendships, and real locations. That’s the kind of credibility that money can’t manufacture, and brands increasingly depend on. Placement in our video positions Peak Performance as a brand that supports the crews actually driving the culture forward, not just the mainstream competition scene.

Partnership Vision

How we'll amplify Peak Performance's brand

Long Form Content

Full-length ski films have real staying power. They get rewatched for seasons, shared between friends, and referenced by athletes and filmmakers, creating long-term organic impressions. This gives Peak an evergreen presence across YouTube, premieres, and festival submissions.

Short Form Content

We’ll deliver Peak-branded cutdowns, athlete clips, behind-the-scenes segments, vertical edits for social, and still photography. Peak receives versatile, authentic assets that depict real use in real conditions.

Authentic Brand Integration

Peak products will be showcased naturally and integrated into the story without loud branding. Clean cinematography, honest visuals, strong music choices, and a grounded approach align with Peak’s identity.

Multi-Platform Distribution

We tailor content for the platforms that matter most today: YouTube, Instagram, and Newschoolers. This helps Peak maximize their exposure and reach a different type of audience

Long-term Partnership

We can collaborate on rail jams, premieres, and community events, providing some value to our community while also building Peak's brand presence throughout the season.

Target Audience Alignment

Supporting CHEF shows Peak is investing in the next wave. Brands that back young crews earn loyalty, relevance, and cultural traction that big-budget campaigns can’t match. It sends a clear message: Peak Performance understands where skiing is going, not just where it’s been.
